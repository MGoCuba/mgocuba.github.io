University of Michigan engineering students explore design in Cuba

About



A cohort of 22 engineering students from the University of Michigan are heading to Havana over Spring Break in February 2017 as part of a new course called "Design in a Resource-Constrained Environment."

This first-of-its-kind engineering program is exposing students to the natural and financial resources in Cuba as they are linked to its import/export economy, as well as the current state of opportunities in Cuban industrial development.

During their time in Cuba, the team has scheduled visits with the International Center for Biotechnology and Genetics, the Havana University of Technology (CUJAE), the national Cuban solar energy initiative (Cuba Solar), and the Center for Molecular Immunology, interspersed with some other cultural exposure. This unique experience will expose students to the challenges of engineering, prototyping, and design in a resource-constrained environment.

Following their return from Cuba, the students will host an exhibit of photographs and other observations from their experiences March 13th and 14th at the Duderstadt Gallery Space in the connector between the Duderstadt Center and Pierpont Commons. A reception is planned Tuesday, March 14th from 3:30 to 5:30PM in the gallery space where select students will be available.
